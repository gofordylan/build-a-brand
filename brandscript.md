# BrandScript: Network School for Businesses

## 1. The Character (Hero)
**Want:** To turn their startup into a real business that makes money

## 2. The Problem
**Villain:** The friction of daily life — the invisible tax of cooking, cleaning, commuting, and managing a living situation that eats hours every day
**External:** Founders spend hours every week on cooking, cleaning, commuting, and managing their living situation instead of building
**Internal:** Frustrated — they know every wasted hour is a competitor gaining ground
**Philosophical:** Building something great is hard enough — daily logistics shouldn't be the thing that slows you down

## 3. The Guide
**Empathy:** "We know what it's like to lose your best hours to things that have nothing to do with your startup"
**Authority:** "We've built the infrastructure so you don't have to — housing, food, fitness, and housekeeping, all handled, all included, already serving founders on the ground"

## 4. The Plan
**Process:**
1. Apply for your team
2. Pick your start date
3. Show up and build — we handle the rest

**Agreement:** No long-term contracts — stay month to month. If it's not working, leave.

## 5. Call to Action
**Direct:** "Bring Your Team to NS"
**Transitional:** "See How Teams Build at NS" — a short video or photo tour of daily life on campus

## 6. Failure
You keep splitting your team's energy between building and surviving. Months pass. Competitors who went all-in ship first. Your runway gets shorter but your product doesn't get closer to revenue.

## 7. Success
You stop running a household and start running a company. Your team is sharper, your product ships faster, and your runway stretches further — because you stopped spending it on everything except the thing that matters.
